WORK

Hudl

Role: Lead Interface Designer
Paradigm: Windows 8

During the 2012-2013 academic year, I worked with six other students to make a Windows 8 application for Hudl, a video software company specializing in tools for athletes and coaches. I served as the lead interface designer on the project, conducting coach interviews, creating mockups, collaborating with Hudl designers, maintaining a set of design principles for my team's work, and writing some of the XAML and C# code needed for the interface. We completely redesigned (and partially recoded) the interface twice during development, but in the end, we were definitely proud of what we managed to put together. We even gained a four-star average rating in the app store.

HDR

Role: Development Manager
Paradigm: Hospital Wayfinding

Over the course of the 2013-2014 academic year, I worked with another student team to design a hospital wayfinding system for HDR. We followed HDR's own Synapse design process, actively scoping the project (and our design principles), empathisizing through core user personas, brainstorming, prototyping, and testing with relevant users. We hit the whiteboards hard, and traveled to several hospitals to get a feel for how confusing navigation can be... especially when you're not feeling your best. At the end of the project, we had an expansive idea catalogue and several working prototypes that leveraged graphic affordance and digital guidance (via Bluetooth, Android, and Arduino systems).

Garmin International

Role: Software Engineering Intern
Paradigm: GPS Trail Watch

During the summer of 2013, I was able to intern at Garmin International in Olathe, Kansas. I served in the outdoor division, working primary on GPS trail watches. In particular, I was tasked with coding temperature-compensated contrast settings for several LCD watch displays and creating a Virtual Partner pacing feature for the Fenix and Tactix watches. Design was primarily conducted via paper mockups, with the feature needing to adhere to existing Garmin standards. After the Virtual Partner was implemented in C, I was able to test it on my own Fenix as I biked around the Garmin campus. It was an awesome experience, and I was very happy that inital feature reviews were positive.
